### **Recruitment Process**  
**Definition**: A systematic approach to attract, evaluate, and select candidates for job positions, involving sequential steps and decision points.  
**Formula**: Not applicable (process flowchart representation).  
**Example**: The process begins with *Search Jobs*, followed by *Prepare Documents*, *Submit Application*, and progresses through *Interview* and *Decision-Making*.

### **Application Submission**  
**Definition**: The act of formally sending job application materials (e.g., resume, cover letter) to an employer.  
**Formula**: Not applicable.  
**Example**: After *Prepare Documents*, candidates *Submit Application* via the job portal.  

---

### **Confirm Application Submission**  
**Definition**: A decision point where the applicant verifies the submission of their application.  
**Formula**:  
$$
\text{Confirm Application Submission} = 
\begin{cases} 
\text{Yes} \rightarrow \text{Forward Application} \\
\text{No} \rightarrow \text{Update Job Portal} 
\end{cases}
$$  
**Example**: If the applicant confirms submission, the application is *Forward Application*; otherwise, the *Job Portal* is updated.  

---

### **Shortlist Applicants**  
**Definition**: Selecting candidates who meet predefined criteria to proceed to the next stage (e.g., interviews).  
**Formula**:  
$$
\text{Shortlist Applicants} = 
\begin{cases} 
\text{Yes} \rightarrow \text{Schedule Interview} \\
\text{No} \rightarrow \text{Notify Applicants} 
\end{cases}
$$  
**Example**: Candidates are *Shortlist Applicants* based on resume review; shortlisted candidates proceed to *Schedule Interview*.  

---

### **Interview Scheduling**  
**Definition**: Coordinating a mutually convenient time for the candidate and interviewer.  
**Formula**: Not applicable.  
**Example**: After *Shortlist Applicants*, the recruiter *Schedule Interview* with the candidate.  

---

### **Make Decision**  
**Definition**: Finalizing whether to hire a candidate after the interview.  
**Formula**:  
$$
\text{Make Decision} = 
\begin{cases} 
\text{Yes} \rightarrow \text{Send Job Offer} \\
\text{No} \rightarrow \text{Create Rejection Email} 
\end{cases}
$$  
**Example**: Post-interview, the hiring team *Make Decision* to either *Send Job Offer* or *Create Rejection Email*.  

---

### **Onboarding Process**  
**Definition**: Integrating a new hire into the organization, including administrative and orientation tasks.  
**Formula**: Not applicable.  
**Example**: After accepting the *Send Job Offer*, the candidate begins *Onboarding Process* with *Send Registration Email*.  

---

### **Update Job Portal**  
**Definition**: Modifying the job portal status to reflect changes in the recruitment process.  
**Formula**: Not applicable.  
**Example**: If *Confirm Application Submission* is "No" or *Make Decision* is "No", the *Job Portal* is updated.  

---

### **Notify Applicants**  
**Definition**: Informing candidates about their application status (e.g., rejection, next steps).  
**Formula**: Not applicable.  
**Example**: Candidates not *Shortlist Applicants* are *Notify Applicants* via email.  

---

### **Process No**  
**Definition**: Terminating the recruitment process for a candidate or job posting.  
**Formula**: Not applicable.  
**Example**: If *Decide Offer* is "No", the process ends with *Process No*.
