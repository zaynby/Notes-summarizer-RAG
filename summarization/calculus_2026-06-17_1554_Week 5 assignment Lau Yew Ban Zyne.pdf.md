### Term: [[Limit]]  
- **Definition**: The value that a function approaches as the input (or independent variable) gets closer to a certain point.  
- **Formula**:  
  $$
  \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L
  $$  
- **Example**:  
  $$
  \lim_{x \to 2} (3x^2) = 3(2)^2 = 12
  $$

---

### Term: [[Derivative]]  
- **Definition**: A measure of the rate of change of a function with respect to its input.  
- **Formula**:  
  $$
  f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}
  $$  
- **Example**:  
  $$
  f(x) = x^3 \implies f'(x) = 3x^2
  $$

---

### Term: [[Integral]]  
- **Definition**: The antiderivative of a function, representing the area under a curve.  
- **Formula**:  
  $$
  \int f(x) \, dx = F(x) + C
  $$  
- **Example**:  
  $$
  \int 2x \, dx = x^2 + C
  $$

---

### Term: [[Sequence]]  
- **Definition**: An ordered list of numbers or terms.  
- **Formula**:  
  $$
  a_n = f(n) \quad \text{for } n = 1, 2, 3, \dots
  $$  
- **Example**:  
  The sequence $ a_n = 2n $ is $ 2, 4, 6, 8, \dots $

### Term: [[Series]]  
- **Definition**: The sum of the terms of a sequence.  
- **Formula**:  
  $$
  S_n = \sum_{k=1}^n a_k
  $$  
- **Example**:
