# SAP S/4HANA Fundamentals  

## Term -> Definition -> Example  

### [[SAP S/4HANA]]  
**Definition**: Next-generation enterprise resource planning (ERP) suite built on the SAP HANA in-memory database, enabling real-time data processing, simplified workflows, and integration across business functions.  
**Example**: A manufacturing company uses SAP S/4HANA to synchronize production schedules, inventory management, and financial reporting in real time.  

---

### [[SAP HANA]]  
**Definition**: In-memory database technology that processes data in columnar storage, enabling high-speed analytics and transactional operations by leveraging parallel processing and compression.  
**Example**: A retailer analyzes customer purchase patterns instantly using SAP HANA’s real-time analytics during peak holiday sales.  

---

### [[SAP Fiori]]  
**Definition**: Unified user experience interface for SAP applications, providing role-specific, mobile-friendly dashboards for transactional, analytical, and contextual tasks.  
**Example**: A procurement manager approves purchase orders via the SAP Fiori app on a tablet, accessing real-time inventory data.  

---

### [[Data Types in ERP Systems]]  
**Definition**:  
- **Organizational Data**: Structural units like company codes, plants, and sales organizations.  
- **Master Data**: Core entities (e.g., customer, material, vendor masters) stored long-term.  
- **Transaction Data**: Records of business events (e.g., sales orders, invoices) referencing master and organizational data.  
**Example**: A sales order (transaction data) links to a customer master (master data) and a company code (organizational data).  

---

### [[Document Flow]]  
**Definition**: Sequence of documents generated during business processes, tracking status changes and ensuring data consistency.  
**Example**: A purchase order (document) triggers a goods receipt and invoice, with each step updating the document flow status in SAP S/4HANA.  

---

### [[Transition to SAP S/4HANA]]  
**Definition**: Methods to migrate to SAP S/4HANA:  
1. **New Implementation (Greenfield)**: Fresh deployment, reengineering processes.  
2. **System Conversion (Brownfield)**: Migrate existing SAP systems to S/4HANA.  
3. **Landscape Transformation**: Consolidate multiple systems into S/4HANA.  
**Example**: A company with legacy ERP systems uses landscape transformation to merge regional systems into a centralized S/4HANA instance.  

---

### [[Optimization Levers]]  
**Definition**: Strategies to enhance SAP S/4HANA performance:  
- **Agility**: Real-time data for flexible decision-making.  
- **Effectiveness**: Streamlined processes (e.g., accelerated material requirements planning).  
- **Efficiency**: Automation and reduced manual steps (e.g., simplified data models).  
**Example**: Real-time inventory management reduces stock-outs and safety stock costs by 30%.  

---

### [[Digital Transformation Drivers]]  
**Definition**: Forces shaping ERP evolution:  
- **Technological**: IoT, Big Data, Cloud, Mobility.  
- **Customer**: Demand for personalized, real-time services.  
**Example**: A logistics firm uses IoT sensors integrated with SAP S/4HANA to track shipments in real time.  

---

### [[Simplified Data Model]]  
**Definition**: Denormalized data structure reducing complexity, eliminating redundant aggregations, and accelerating queries.  
**Example**: A sales document now impacts 4 tables instead of 26, cutting processing time by 70%.  

---

### [[SAP S/4HANA Deployment Options]]  
**Definition**:  
- **On-Premise**: Customer-controlled infrastructure with traditional licensing.  
- **Cloud**: Subscription-based, SAP-maintained with quarterly updates.  
**Example**: A mid-sized company opts for cloud deployment to reduce IT maintenance costs.
